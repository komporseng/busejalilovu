The 5 longest Tennis Matches of all time

 Posted on June 2, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  474 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Tennis is a sport known for its thrilling matches, and some of the most memorable moments in the sport's history have come from the epic battles that have lasted for hours on end. These marathon encounters have captivated audiences and left an indelible mark on the sport. Here, we take a look at the five longest tennis matches of all time, each with its own unique story and enduring legacy.

1. John Isner vs. Nicolas Mahut - Wimbledon 2010

The match between John Isner and Nicolas Mahut at Wimbledon in 2010 holds the record for the longest tennis match in history. Lasting a staggering 11 hours and 5 minutes over three days, the match set records for the most games in a set (138) and the most games in a match (183). Isner eventually emerged victorious with a final score of 6–4, 3–6, 6–7(7), 7–6(3), 70–68.

2. Fabrice Santoro vs. Arnaud Clement - French Open 2004

The 2004 French Open witnessed an extraordinary battle between Fabrice Santoro and Arnaud Clement, lasting 6 hours and 33 minutes. Santoro, known for his unorthodox playing style, outlasted Clement to claim victory with a final score of 6–4, 6–3, 6–7(5), 3–6, 16–14. This match showcased incredible resilience and endurance from both players, captivating the tennis world.

3. Jo-Wilfried Tsonga vs. Guillermo Canas - Australian Open 2007

Another memorable marathon match took place at the 2007 Australian Open when Jo-Wilfried Tsonga faced Guillermo Canas. Lasting 6 hours and 9 minutes, the match tested the physical and mental limits of both players. Tsonga eventually emerged triumphant with a final score of 6–7(8), 6–4, 6–7(4), 6–4, 8–6, showcasing his remarkable determination and stamina.

4. Leonardo Mayer vs. João Souza - Davis Cup 2015

The 2015 Davis Cup featured an epic encounter between Leonardo Mayer and João Souza, lasting 6 hours and 43 minutes. Mayer displayed incredible fortitude to outlast Souza, ultimately securing victory with a final score of 7–6(4), 7–6(5), 5–7, 5–7, 15–13. This match exemplified the grueling nature of Davis Cup competition and the unwavering commitment of the players.

5. Novak Djokovic vs. Rafael Nadal - Australian Open 2012

In the 2012 Australian Open, tennis fans witnessed a titanic struggle between Novak Djokovic and Rafael Nadal, lasting 5 hours and 53 minutes. The match tested the physical and mental reserves of both players as they battled for every point. Djokovic ultimately emerged victorious with a final score of 5–7, 6–4, 6–2, 6–7(5), 7–5, cementing his reputation as a tenacious competitor.

These five matches stand as testaments to the extraordinary physical and mental demands of professional tennis. They showcase the resilience, determination, and unwavering spirit of the players who endured grueling battles on the court. These matches have etched their place in tennis history, captivating fans and inspiring future generations of players to push their limits in pursuit of greatness

Joy Behar was born on October 7, 1942, in Williamsburg, Brooklyn, New York,

Gino Occhiuto, her father, was a truck driver for the Coca Cola Company, and her mother, Rose Occhiuto née Carbone, was a seamstress.

She was a part of ABC’s The View original roster for 16 years before returning as co-host in September 2015 for the show’s three most recent seasons. Behar is the recipient of three MAC awards, a CableACE award, and a 2000 GLAAD award for her longstanding connection with the LGBT community, in addition to the 2009 Daytime Emmy® Award for Outstanding Talk Show Host.

Behar has presented many of her own shows, such as a  call-in radio program on WABC, The Joy Behar Show on HLN, and Say Anything on Current TV.

Her film credits include Nora Ephron’s This is My Life and Woody Allen’s Manhattan  Murder Mystery.

Behar appeared in Nicky Silver’s play The Food Chain, for which she received critical praise, as well as the critically acclaimed piece The Vagina Monologues.

At the Cherry Lane Theatre, she also developed and performed her own solo performance, Me, My Mouth, and I. Behar has published five novels, two of which are for children.

The Great Gasbag: An A-to-Z Study Guide to Surviving Trump World, her most recent book, was published in October 2017.
